Prophets of the Heart · Unit 1
Ibrahim
and the Fire
A story about love bigger than any flame
Page 1 · The Big Question
Long, long ago, in a land of golden sand and a million stars,
there lived a boy named Ibrahim.
Every night he sat alone and looked up at the sky.
A question burned inside him, too big to ignore:
"Who made all of this?"
Page 2 · The Search
He saw the bright stars and thought: "Maybe that is my Lord!"
But when morning came — the stars faded away.
He watched the moon sail across the sky.
When it vanished, he said: "My Lord does not disappear."
"My Lord is the One who made them all —
and is always here."
Page 3 · The Stone Gods
Ibrahim's father carved statues from stone — tall, silent, and cold.
People brought them flowers and bowed before them.
But Ibrahim looked into their stone eyes and felt something ache inside.
"They cannot see you.
They cannot hear you.
They are only rock."
Page 4 · The Brave Act
One day, while everyone was at a festival, Ibrahim walked into the empty temple.
He smashed the statues — all but the biggest one.
He hung the axe on the big idol's shoulder and waited.
When the people returned shouting, "Who did this?!"
"Ask him. He has the axe."
Page 5 · The Great Fire
The king was furious. He ordered the biggest fire anyone had ever seen.
The flames leapt so high that birds fled from the heat.
Everyone trembled — everyone except Ibrahim.
يَا نَارُ كُونِي بَرْدًا وَسَلَامًا عَلَىٰ إِبْرَاهِيمَ
"O fire — be coolness and safety for Ibrahim."
— Allah's words, Quran 21:69
Page 6 · The "No" That Was Love
Just before Ibrahim was thrown in, the angel Jibreel came down from the sky.
"Do you need anything? I can help you."
Ibrahim felt something glowing in his chest — not fear, but the most peaceful feeling he had ever known.
"From you? No.
My Lord knows what I need.
He is enough for me."
Page 7 · The Garden Inside the Fire
Allah spoke to the fire:
"Be cool. Be safe.
For Ibrahim."
The fire listened.
It burned only his chains — and set him free.
Ibrahim walked out calm and bright.
His face glowed. His clothes smelled of flowers, not smoke.
Page 8 · The Secret of the Khalil
Do you know why Allah called Ibrahim "Al-Khalil"?
Khalil doesn't just mean "friend." It means something deeper —
like colour soaking all the way through a cloth until you cannot separate them.
Allah's love soaked all the way through Ibrahim.
That is why the fire could not touch him.
Page 9 · What Is Your Fire?
Ibrahim's fire was enormous. Yours might look smaller — but it is still a fire.
Maybe it is raising your hand when you're not sure,
saying sorry when it feels too hard,
or being kind when no one else is.
You have the same heart as Ibrahim.
What is your fire?
✦ The End
Ibrahim walked out of the fire
as if he had been in a garden.
He showed us what no fire can burn:
Love for Allah is the biggest thing there is.
Bigger than any flame.
May your heart always carry that love. 🌟
