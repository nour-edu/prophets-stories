🌙 Jonah and His People
Long ago, in a great city called Nineveh, people had forgotten Allah.

They bowed to statues and refused to listen.

Allah sent a man named Jonah — peace be upon him — to call them back. Jonah called and called. But the people laughed and turned away.

And Jonah's heart grew heavy.
Ibn ʿArabī teaches: the wisdom of Jonah is the wisdom of the nafas — the breath and the soul. Every soul carries the breath of the Merciful inside it. Even when people forget Allah, that breath never leaves them.
🚢 Jonah Leaves
Jonah became frustrated. He felt his people would never listen.

So he left — without Allah's permission. He walked to the sea and boarded a ship, deciding his mission was over.

But a prophet cannot simply walk away from what Allah has given him.

The sea knew something Jonah did not yet know.
Ibn ʿArabī teaches: we cannot run away from our own soul. Wherever Jonah went, Allah was already there — waiting, not with anger, but with a lesson wrapped in mercy.

⛈️ The Storm!
A terrible storm came. The waves grew huge. The ship was going to sink!

The sailors were afraid. They thought the storm was a punishment — someone on the ship had done wrong.

They drew lots to find out who. "And he drew lots and was among the losers." (Quran 37:141)

The lot fell to Jonah. Three times. Each time — Jonah.

And so he jumped into the sea — and a great whale swallowed him whole.
Ibn ʿArabī says: Allah uses the whole universe to speak to His beloved ones. Even a storm, even a lot drawn by chance — is Allah calling you back to yourself.

🐋 Three Darknesses
Jonah was thrown into the sea — and a great whale swallowed him whole.

The Quran tells us he was surrounded by three darknesses: the darkness of the night, the darkness of the deep sea, and the darkness of the whale's belly.

He was alone. Completely alone.

Or so it seemed.
Ibn ʿArabī says: the belly of the whale is an image of the soul's deepest trial. When everything is dark and silent — that is when Allah is most near. The darkness is not a punishment. It is a conversation.
🤲 The Prayer in the Dark
In the deepest darkness, Jonah remembered Allah.

And he called out — with his whole heart:

"There is no god but You — Glory be to You — I have certainly been among the wrongdoers." (Quran 21:87)

He did not ask for anything. He only remembered Allah and admitted his mistake.

And that was enough.
Ibn ʿArabī says: the most powerful prayer is not a request — it is a recognition. When the soul says laa ilaaha illa anta — "there is no god but You" — the whole universe breathes with it.
🌅 Allah Answers!
Allah heard Jonah's prayer.

The Quran says: "So We responded to him and rescued him from distress. And thus do We save the believers." (Quran 21:88)

The whale cast Jonah onto the shore — exhausted, but alive. Free.

From the darkest place in the world — to the open air. To light. To life.
Ibn ʿArabī teaches: the moment the soul truly says laa ilaaha illa anta — no power in creation can hold it anymore. Not darkness, not a whale, not despair.

🌿 The Gourd Tree
The whale cast Jonah onto a bare, open shore — "while he was sick" (Quran 37:145). His skin was raw and burned. He was exhausted, in pain, with no shade and no food.

So Allah caused a gourd vine to grow over him — miraculously, instantly. A wild goat came morning and evening. He healed slowly, gently.

He went from the darkest belly in the world — to this. That is mercy.
Ibn ʿArabī teaches: Allah's mercy doesn't stop at rescue. It continues — with shade, rest, and tenderness. This is the nafas al-Rahman — the Breath of the Merciful — flowing without end.
✨ A Whole City Turns to Allah!
While Jonah was still in the whale — something extraordinary happened back in Nineveh.

The people saw signs of punishment approaching. The sky changed. Fear filled their hearts. And without waiting for Jonah to return — they repented on their own.

All of them. Men, women, children — they went out together and cried to Allah. And Allah lifted the punishment.

Jonah returned to find his people already turned toward Allah. The mission he thought had failed — was already complete.

The Quran says: "And We sent him to a hundred thousand or more — and they believed." (Quran 37:147-148)
Ibn ʿArabī teaches: the prayer said in total darkness, in the belly of a whale — moved an entire city to light. That is the power of a soul that truly remembers Allah.

Your Three Darknesses.

Jonah was in three darknesses at once. Your darknesses are different — but they are still real.

Maybe your darkness is a moment when you feel completely alone. Or when you made a mistake and feel ashamed. Or when you are so far from Allah that you don't know how to come back.

That heavy, dark feeling? That is your whale.

Jonah's way out was not a ladder or a rope. It was a word.

Laa ilaaha illa anta Subhaanaka Inni kuntu minaz-zaalimeen

In your darkness — you have the same key Jonah had.

Say Soob-haa-na-llaah — Allah is perfect and greater than anything that is hurting me.
Say Laa ilaaha illa Allah — there is nothing and no one who can save me except Him.
Tell Him: I am weak. I need You. Without You, I am nothing.

That honesty — that is your key. That is what opened the whale.

Laa i-laa-ha il-laa an-ta · sub-haa-na-ka · in-ni kun-tu mi-naz-zaa-li-meen
JONAH'S DU'A — QURAN 21:87
"There is no god but You — Glory be to You — I have certainly been among the wrongdoers."

The Prophet ﷺ said: "No Muslim ever calls upon Allah with these words for anything, ever, except that Allah will respond to him." (At-Tirmidhi)

This is Jonah's gift to you — a key that opens every locked door, in every darkness.
Ibn ʿArabī teaches: this du'a works because it has three movements — recognizing Allah's oneness, glorifying Him, and admitting our own smallness. When the soul is in that position, it is exactly where Allah wants it to be.
