🏛️ The Mighty People of Aad
After Prophet Noah, Allah blessed his descendants and they spread across the earth. One great nation was called the Aad.

They were the superpower of their time — enormous in height, extraordinary in strength, rulers of a vast land of green gardens and towering stone cities.

The Quran says: "Have you not seen what your Lord did with Aad — with Iram of the lofty pillars, the likes of whom had never been created in the land?" (Quran 89:6-8)

They had everything. And they knew it.
Ibn ʿArabī says: Hud teaches us something beautiful — that you learn about Allah not just by reading about Him, but by walking with Him every day. Every step you take when things are hard, every time you stay honest even when it is difficult — that is how your heart gets closer to Allah.

😤 Pride and Forgetting
The Aad had everything — strength, wealth, gardens, palaces.

But their power made them proud. They built their towering monuments not to thank Allah — but to show off. "Who is greater than us in strength?" they boasted. (Quran 41:15)

They knew Allah existed. But they chose to worship idols alongside Him — gods made of stone they carved with their own hands.

And they began to oppress the weak. Justice disappeared. Arrogance ruled.
Ibn ʿArabī says: when someone is very proud — thinking only about how strong they are, how big their house is, how great they are — their heart gets too full of themselves to have room for Allah. The Aad's hearts were so full of pride, there was no space left for love of Allah.
🗣️ Hud Calls His People
So Allah sent a prophet from among the Aad themselves — their own brother, their own family.

His name was Hud.

He stood among them and called: "O my people! Worship Allah — you have no god other than Him. Will you not then fear Him?" (Quran 7:65)

He asked nothing in return: "I do not ask you for any reward. My reward is only from the One who created me." (Quran 11:51)

He was one of them. He loved them. And he called them, day after day.
Ibn ʿArabī says: Hud shows us that doing good — even when it is really, really hard — brings you closer to Allah. You don't have to be perfect. You just have to keep going, one day at a time.
😤 They Mocked Him — and the Years Passed
The chiefs of the Aad refused. They called Hud a fool and a liar:

"Indeed, we see you in foolishness, and indeed, we think you are of the liars." (Quran 7:66)

Hud kept calling. And the years passed.

But with each passing year, the Aad grew prouder, more obstinate, more tyrannical. They mocked Hud more. They built more towers — not out of gratitude, but out of arrogance. They oppressed the weak more. They drifted further from Allah.

And still Hud called. Because a prophet never stops.
Ibn ʿArabī says: if someone laughs at you for doing what is right — that is not a sign you are wrong. It is a sign you are doing something important. Hud kept going every single day. And that is one of the bravest things anyone can ever do.
💪 "Do Your Worst. I Trust Allah."
They said the most hurtful thing — that their stone gods had made Hud go crazy. But Hud stood tall and declared something extraordinary:

"I call Allah to witness — and you witness too — that I reject everything you worship beside Him. So all of you plot against me, without delay!" (Quran 11:54-55)

He was fearless. Not because he was strong — but because he trusted Allah completely.

"I place my trust in Allah — my Lord and your Lord. There is no living creature that is not completely under His control." (Quran 11:56)
Ibn ʿArabī says: trusting Allah doesn't mean you are not scared. It means that even when you ARE scared, you remember: Allah is holding me. That is what Hud felt. And that feeling — that Allah is always with you — makes you braver than any army.
🏜️
Hud was called a fool by his whole people — his own family, his own tribe.

If everyone around you called you a fool for doing what is right — what would YOU do? 🤔

☁️ "A Cloud! It's Bringing Us Rain!"
Then, a massive dark cloud appeared on the horizon, moving toward the valley of the Aad.

The people saw it and were filled with joy. They had been suffering from drought. Finally — rain!

"This is a cloud bringing us rain!" they cried happily.

But the Quran tells us: "No — it was what they had asked to hasten — a wind in which there is a painful punishment." (Quran 46:24)

What they thought was their blessing — was the truth they had refused to hear, finally arriving.
Ibn ʿArabī says: if you spend too long pretending something wrong is okay, you start to really believe it. The Aad had forgotten Allah for so long that even when the sign of punishment came, they thought it was a gift. That is why we should always stay close to Allah — so we never lose our way of seeing.
🌪️ Seven Nights and Eight Days
A fierce, screaming wind came upon the Aad.

"And as for Aad — they were destroyed by a screaming, violent wind, which Allah imposed upon them for seven nights and eight days in succession." (Quran 69:6-7)

Everything the Aad had built — their towers, their palaces, their monuments of pride — was destroyed. The Quran says they were left like hollow trunks of fallen palm trees.

Their great strength could not save them. Their tall towers could not shelter them. "Who is greater than us?" — the wind answered.
Ibn ʿArabī says: Allah was not being mean to the Aad. He gave them so much time — so many chances. He sent them Hud. He gave them warnings. But they kept choosing their towers over Allah. And here is the truth: you cannot build anything higher than Allah. The biggest tower in the world is still smaller than a single word of His.
🌿 Hud and the Believers — Safe
When the punishment came, Hud and the believers who followed him took refuge in an open enclosure — a simple open area, nothing but stakes and rope.

And a miracle happened.

The fierce wind that was collapsing stone towers and palaces entered the enclosure gently. It could not touch them.

Hud and his companions sat in peace — while the storm raged all around them.

Then, after the destruction, they migrated to Hadramaut — a peaceful town in southern Arabia — where they lived worshipping Allah in peace.
Ibn ʿArabī says: Hud and his friends were not protected by thick walls or tall towers. They were protected by Allah. And that is the safest place in the whole world — not inside a palace, but inside Allah's care.

🏚️ Nothing Remained Except Their Dwellings
When the seven nights and eight days were over — the Aad were gone.

The Quran says: "Destroying everything by command of its Lord — and they became so that nothing was seen of them except their dwellings." (Quran 46:25)

Their towers — buried under sand. Their palaces — rubble. Their strength — gone like dust in the wind.

Today, in the region of southern Arabia, there are ancient ruins that local people still call Dar Aad — the abode of Aad. A reminder that remains in the earth.

Allah says: "Indeed in that is a sign — but most of them are not believers." (Quran 26:139)
Ibn ʿArabī says: the ruins of the Aad are like a message in the sand that says: what did you build your life on? Things that last — like love, honesty, and trust in Allah — or things that fall — like showing off and being proud?
🏗️
What Are You Building?

The Aad built towers of stone. They built to show off — not to thank Allah.

Every time you do something to show off, to be better than others, to make people say "wow" — you are building a tower of the Aad.

That tower will not protect you when the wind comes.

Hud built something different. He built trust.

Every time you do something for Allah — even when no one is watching, even when people laugh, even when it is hard — you are building something the wind cannot touch.

What are you building today?

💭 Think of one thing you did this week.
Did you do it to show off — or did you do it for Allah?

That is the question Hud's story asks you every day.

Hud's Gift to Us
Hud walked among his own people — his own family — and called them back to Allah.

They laughed. The years passed. They grew prouder.

And Hud kept walking. Day after day. Step by step.

Because that is what trust looks like — not a feeling. A walk.
💭 A question just for you...

Is there something right that you keep doing — even when people don't notice? Even when it is hard?

That is your walk with Allah.
Keep going. Hud kept going too. 🏜️
